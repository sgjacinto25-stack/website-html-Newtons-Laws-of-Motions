Newton's First Law of Motion

The Law of Inertia

Newton's First Law of Motion is one of the three fundamental laws of physics developed by Sir Isaac Newton. It explains how objects behave when forces act on them. The first law is commonly called the Law of Inertia.

Newton's first law of motion, often called the law of inertia, states that an object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion with the same speed and in the same direction unless acted upon by an unbalanced external force. This means objects resist changes to their state of motion.

Before Newton introduced this idea, many people believed that objects needed a constant force to keep moving. Newton showed that this belief was incorrect. Objects only stop moving because forces such as friction and air resistance act on them.

Understanding Inertia

Inertia is the inherent physical property of matter that resists changes in its state of motion or rest, as defined by Newton’s first law. Objects with more mass possess greater inertia, requiring more force to accelerate or decelerate. It causes resting objects to stay put and moving objects to continue at a constant velocity.

Mass determines an object's inertia because they are directly proportional. This means that the greater the mass of an object, the greater its resistance to changes in motion. Objects with higher inertia are more difficult to accelerate, slow down, or change direction.

Real-Life Examples

Example Explanation Picture
A book on a table A book resting on a table will stay at rest unless a force pushes or moves it.
Rolling soccer ball When a soccer ball is kicked, it keeps moving because of inertia until friction slows it down.
Passengers in a car When a car suddenly stops, passengers move forward because their bodies continue moving due to inertia.

Key Points

  • Newton's First Law of Motion is one of the three fundamental laws developed by Isaac Newton.
  • It is also called the Law of Inertia.
  • The law states that an object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion at the same speed and direction unless acted upon by an unbalanced external force.
  • Objects naturally resist changes in their state of motion.
  • Inertia is the property of matter that resists changes in motion or rest.
